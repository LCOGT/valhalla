Calls for Proposals

LCO's year is subdivided into two observing semesters, each 6 months long. Calls for proposals are issued each semester. Three types of proposals can be submitted through this LCO Observing Portal:

  • Standard proposals are accepted each semester from astronomers affiliated with LCO's non-guaranteed-time partners: UCSB, U. Colorado, and IPAC at Caltech.
  • Key Project proposals are accepted annually from astronomers affiliated with LCO's Science Collaboration partners (guaranteed and non-guaranteed-time).
  • Director's Discretionary Time proposals are accepted at any time from astronomers affiliated with LCO's Science Collaboration institutions.
Please refer to the LCO website for more information on the time allocation process.

Standard proposals from astronomers affiliated with LCO's Science Collaboration institutions are accepted every semester, but each institution allocates its share of time according to its own TAC procedure. Proposals for Science Collaboration time should not be submitted through this portal.

If you are composing a proposal for the LCO network for the first time, we recommend that you consult our guidelines for writing proposals, as well as the "Getting Started" Guide. Information about the telescopes, instruments, data handling and quality, scheduling and other technical aspects of the network is available on the observatory capabilities pages.
